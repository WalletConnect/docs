WalletConnect Pay works with **every WalletConnect-compatible wallet**. Any wallet that has integrated WalletConnect — over 700 today — can be used by a buyer to pay a WCP merchant. There is no separate WCP-only wallet allow-list.

For the full, always-current directory, point your customers at [WalletGuide](https://walletguide.walletconnect.network/). Use the [Certified filter](https://walletguide.walletconnect.network/?badge_type=certified) for the subset of wallets that have been reviewed against WalletConnect's highest standards.

## Solana wallets

Phantom and Solflare are the two most-used Solana wallets and both work with WCP via the WalletConnect protocol. They are not part of the Certified program today, but they cover the Solana buyer base that the EVM-focused Certified wallets do not.

## Wallet support is chain-dependent

<Warning>
Wallet support is chain-dependent. Even when a wallet is Certified or listed on WalletGuide, it only works on the chains the wallet itself supports. When picking a wallet for a specific buyer cohort, always check the wallet's chain coverage against the chains your merchant accepts. See [Token and Chain Coverage](/payments/token-and-chain-coverage) for the chains WCP accepts today.
</Warning>

For example, Rainbow and Uniswap Wallet are EVM-only — they cannot sign Solana transactions even though they are Certified. A merchant accepting USDC on Solana would need their buyer to use a Solana-capable wallet such as Phantom, Solflare, OKX Wallet, or Bitget Wallet.

## What does "Certified" mean?

A wallet is WalletConnect Certified when it ranks in the top 50 wallets by network usage and passes review across security, UX, compliance, multi-chain support (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana where applicable), link-mode integration, and 1-click wallet authentication. The full criteria are published on the [WalletConnect blog](https://walletconnect.com/blog/introducing-walletguide-and-walletconnect-certified).
